Singular Sheep Should be Called Shoop

Opinion Piece by Karen Jin


Sheep or Shoop?

The English language is oftentimes confusing for those learning the language, sometimes even for native speakers, puzzled by its inconsistent grammar rules and interesting slang. Today, I am addressing an issue I personally have with the English grammar rules for plural words, which I'm certain you'll find agreeable.

The singular for sheep should be called shoop. Before you call me crazy, let me walk you through my sound reasoning. Below, I have replicated a table that details standard rules for forming plurals [1]:

    As you can see, there are a variety of rules for changing the singular tense of a noun to plural. For most words, you just add an "s" to the end of the word to make it plural. However, as explained on Grammarly, there are many exceptions to this rule:

    Irregular plural nouns are nouns that do not become plural by adding -s or -es, as most nouns in the English language do. You're probably familiar with many of these already...There are hundreds of irregular plural nouns, and in truth, you must memorize them through reading and speaking. There are, however, some common patterns to look out for. [2]
    One such pattern is with words that have "oo" double vowels: such as tooth, goose, foot, etc. For these irregular nouns, there is a vowel change from "oo" to "ee" - tooth becomes teeth, goose becomes geese, foot becomes feet, etc.

    I know what you're going to say: sheep is not the same as these irregular nouns, it has "ee" and not "oo" in the word, so it should instead continue to follow the standard plural rules for "ee" nouns. Many "ee" words follow the standard rule of most nouns in adding an "s" at the end, such queen to queens, bee to bees, green to greens, etc. However, the current plural form of sheep does not even follow this rule! The irregular noun sheep's plural form is also sheep - how confusing is that!!! Are you talking about a single sheep or multiple sheep? I DON'T KNOW!

    Now that we have reached a consensus that one of the tenses of sheep should be changed to avoid further confusion, why should the singular of sheep be shoop instead of the plural tense of sheep be sheeps? Perhaps you think the plural of sheep should be sheeps to follow the precedent set by all the other "ee" nouns. However, since we are working to change the tense of sheep anyways, why give up the chance to say the word SHOOP?! The gratification of saying the word shoop to refer to singular sheep is also supported by the existing "oo" rule we previously mentioned, so you would still be grammatically correct!

    Thank you.
